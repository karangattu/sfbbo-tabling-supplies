SFBBO Tabling Supply Suggestions

A consolidated summary of ideas proposed to enhance the effectiveness of SFBBO's outreach tables. The staff will consider these suggestions for the 2026 outreach budget.

Update Banner & Tablecloth
Refresh the primary banner to be more modern and artistic. Procure a new, modern tablecloth with the updated logo, potentially an adjustable one from a site like 4imprint.com.
Elaine, Jan
Digital Video/Photo Display
Use a laptop or digital frame to play a short (2-minute) rolling video about SFBBO's mission. A rotating digital photo display could also showcase the wide range of projects.
Chris, Jan
Enlarged Bay Area Project Map
Display an enlarged, poster-sized map of the Bay Area to use as a conversation starter. A map showing SFBBO's active project sites already exists in a handout and could be adapted.
Lisa, Debbie

Interactive Bird ID Game
Instead of a single-user scope, use three large photos of shorebirds (e.g., Snowy Plover, Killdeer, Long-billed Curlew) so groups can identify field marks together.
Lisa
Expanded Migration Map
Enhance the existing migration map to include birds SFBBO researches and incorporate MOTUS data. Add "jaw dropping" fun facts that passersby can read. The display could be made more interactive with laminated maps and velcro to show complex migration routes.
Jan, Lisa
Banding & MOTUS Display
Create a unified display featuring: a shortened, modified mist net from the station's old stock, potentially with a fake bird in it; a string of different-sized bird bands or old/problem bands if possible; and a photo/map of MOTUS towers.
Jan, Lisa, Debbie
Matching Games
Create a game where participants match program pins to a mission poster. Another idea is a larger, "lift the flap" style game matching photos of birds to their unique nest types (e.g., cup, scrape, hanging).
Debbie, Jan

Nest & Egg Display
Create a display with real bird nests, which could be sourced from volunteers or rehab centers. The display could also feature a model, like a fake gull on a nest with eggs, and subtle audio of bird calls.
Jan, Lisa
Habitat Restoration Props
Use simple props like a hand shovel, gloves, and fake grasses on the table to initiate conversations about habitat restoration and volunteer opportunities.
Lisa
Bird Specimen Display
Use the organization's collection of donated dead birds for a "show and tell" display. Staff could be asked about using bird skins that are currently destined for storage, which could be displayed in protective tubes.
Jan, Debbie
Burrowing Owl Display
Use a photo of a Burrowing Owl to create an engaging educational moment by asking visitors where owls live.
Lisa
Hand-holdable Models
Have secured, a hand-holdable models of different bird beaks and feet to demonstrate adaptation.
Jan
