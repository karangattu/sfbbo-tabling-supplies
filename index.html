SFBBO Tabling Supply Suggestions

A consolidated summary of ideas proposed to enhance the effectiveness of SFBBO's outreach tables. The staff will consider these suggestions for the 2026 outreach budget.

Suggestion Category Consolidated Idea Details and Notes Proposed By
Signage & Visual Branding Update Banner & Tablecloth Refresh the primary banner to be more modern and artistic. Procure a new, modern tablecloth with the updated logo, potentially an adjustable one from a site like 4imprint.com. Elaine, Jan
Signage & Visual Branding Digital Video/Photo Display Use a laptop or digital frame to play a short (2-minute) rolling video about SFBBO's mission. A rotating digital photo display could also showcase the wide range of projects. Chris, Jan
Signage & Visual Branding Enlarged Bay Area Project Map Display an enlarged, poster-sized map of the Bay Area to use as a conversation starter. A map showing SFBBO's active project sites already exists in a handout and could be adapted. Lisa, Debbie
Interactive Displays & Games Interactive Bird ID Game Instead of a single-user scope, use three large photos of shorebirds (e.g., Snowy Plover, Killdeer, Long-billed Curlew) so groups can identify field marks together. Lisa
Interactive Displays & Games Expanded Migration Map Enhance the existing migration map to include birds SFBBO researches and incorporate MOTUS data. Add "jaw dropping" fun facts that passersby can read. The display could be made more interactive with laminated maps and velcro to show complex migration routes. Jan, Lisa
Interactive Displays & Games Banding & MOTUS Display Create a unified display featuring: a shortened, modified mist net from the station's old stock, potentially with a fake bird in it; a string of different-sized bird bands or old/problem bands if possible; and a photo/map of MOTUS towers. Jan, Lisa, Debbie
Interactive Displays & Games Matching Games Create a game where participants match program pins to a mission poster. Another idea is a larger, "lift the flap" style game matching photos of birds to their unique nest types (e.g., cup, scrape, hanging). Debbie, Jan
Physical Models & Specimens Nest & Egg Display Create a display with real bird nests, which could be sourced from volunteers or rehab centers. The display could also feature a model, like a fake gull on a nest with eggs, and subtle audio of bird calls. Jan, Lisa
Physical Models & Specimens Habitat Restoration Props Use simple props like a hand shovel, gloves, and fake grasses on the table to initiate conversations about habitat restoration and volunteer opportunities. Lisa
Physical Models & Specimens Bird Specimen Display Use the organization's collection of donated dead birds for a "show and tell" display. Staff could be asked about using bird skins that are currently destined for storage, which could be displayed in protective tubes. Jan, Debbie
Physical Models & Specimens Burrowing Owl Display Use a photo of a Burrowing Owl to create an engaging educational moment by asking visitors where owls live. Lisa
Physical Models & Specimens Hand-holdable Models Have secured, hand-holdable models of different bird beaks and feet to demonstrate adaptation. Jan
Giveaways Stickers & Buttons Produce stickers and buttons, as people enjoy them. New stickers could be created using popular designs from SFBBO's long history of t-shirts. Jan
Giveaways 3D-Printed Items As a unique and inexpensive giveaway, produce 3D-printed items like bird whistles. Elaine
Printed Materials Informational Handouts Develop one-page summaries for specific projects. Consider a smaller, postcard-sized handout with project thumbnails and contact/social media info. Jan
Printed Materials FAQ & Business Cards Revive and update a master FAQ sheet for common questions, especially regarding youth volunteer opportunities. Have general business cards available that point to an outreach contact. Jan
